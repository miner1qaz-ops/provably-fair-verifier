How MoCHi fairness works (in plain English)

Before you pull, MoCHi locks a secret ("server seed") and shows you only its fingerprint (a SHA-256 hash). It cannot change the secret after that, or the fingerprint would change. Your pull is then decided by three ingredients:

🔑 Server seedMoCHi's secret. Locked before you pull, revealed after you rotate.
🎲 Lucky phraseYour client seed. Yours to set.
#️⃣ NonceThe pull number: 0, 1, 2… (just a counter).

roll = SHA-256("server_seed : client_seed : nonce") → a number 0–9999 → rarity tier (R / SR / SSR / SUR / EX)

After you rotate your seed, MoCHi reveals the plaintext secret. You can then re-do the math yourself — here, or with any SHA-256 calculator — and confirm the result matches. Because SHA-256 is one-way and the secret was locked before your pull, nobody could have rigged it.

How to get these numbers from MoCHi

  1. Open a pack on getmochi.fun. You can't verify it yet — the secret is still locked.
  2. Go to the verify page → "Seeds" tab → "Rotate Server Seed" and confirm. This reveals the old secret.
  3. Open the "History" tab and click the pull you want to check. It shows the revealed server seed, your client seed, the nonce, the roll hash, roll value and base rarity.
  4. Paste them above and hit Verify. Green ✅ = honest pull.

Tip: you can verify any past pull this way — not just the latest one.

What this can & can't prove

✅ Can prove

The rarity tier of your pull (R / SR / SSR / SUR / EX) was decided honestly from (server seed + your phrase + nonce), and MoCHi did not swap its committed secret. That is the provably-fair claim.

🟡 Cannot prove (stated openly)

• Which specific card you got within that tier — that pick depends on live inventory and is not part of the hash. (This is the same transparency gap every gacha has.)

• The Fortune "soft pity" upgrade — its hash needs pity-counter values that aren't included in the pull data, so it is server-attested rather than independently reproducible. Hard-pity upgrades are deterministic (no hash).

In short: provably-fair rarity, honestly scoped — never "provably-fair everything."
